Javanese Culture

"The characteristics and knowledge of a particular group of people, encompassing language, religion, cuisine, social habits, music and arts."

Literatures

Javanese literature tradition is among the earliest and the oldest surviving literature traditions in Indonesia such as Wayang Kulit, a traditional form of puppet-shadow play originally found in the cultures of Java and Bali in Indonesia.

Spirituality & Structures

All the new religions were not taken literally but instead interpreted by the Javanese according to the Javanese traditional values, creating a new set of religious beliefs unique to local culture.

Ceremonies

Details of the ceremonies differ from one community to the other. Many traditional Javanese customs or festivals such as meditation, slametan, naloni mitoni, patangpuluhdinanan, nyatus and nyewu have their roots in the Kebatinan belief and modify them accordingly.

People, Art & Masterpiece

The Javanese developed over the centuries a rich artistic tradition that can be splitted in two parts, an art complex that came to be essentially associated to the courts of Java as well ass the “lower” popular arts such as “visual aspects” of the Javanese culture are the joglo architecture as well as the art of wax and dye textile (batik).

Arts of the court: Wayang Kulit. Internationaly known as a striking feature of the Javanese art. The puppeteer (dalang) tells myths (usually from the Javanese version of the Mahabharata or the Ramayana) by acting, singing and the song of gamelan (Javanese percussion orchestra) accompanying him.

Religion & Beliefs

Java is depicted at its origin as covered with primeval forests and without inhabitants but the native spirits. As natives populated the island, cleared the forests for ricefields and set up villages, the spirits fled into the craters of volcanoes, to the woods or to the depths of the Southern Sea.

In today's standards, the “gravity center” of the Javanese society has obviously shifted towards more orthodoxy but in their private life, many Javanese still practice tolerances in order to illustrate that religious matters is not much concerned as long as we as a human being still keep intact of our sanity's and kindness.
